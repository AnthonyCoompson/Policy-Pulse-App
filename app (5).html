Quick Start Guide
Get up and running with PolicyPulse in minutes
Quick Start Guide
1

Get Your Free Groq API Key

Visit console.groq.com → sign up → API Keys → Create Key. Free, no credit card. Sign in with Google, click "Create API Key". Paste it in the prompt when PolicyPulse loads to enable Ask AI.

2

Configure Your Sources

Go to Scraper → Sources to add or remove government/policy news sources. Click Scrape Now to fetch new articles on demand.

3

Set Up Your Keyword Watchlist

In Scraper → Search & Archive, add keywords like "pharmacare", "TRC", "DRIPA". Articles matching your keywords are surfaced automatically in the Dashboard Recommended panel.

4

Review the Article Feed

Click any article headline ↗ to open the source article in a new tab. Use ✓ Mark Read to track what you've reviewed, and ↩ Mark Unread to undo. Use + Add to Digest to stage an article.

5

Build & Send Your Digest

Staged articles auto-organize in Digest → Staging Area. Add "Why This Matters" notes, then click Send Digest, Copy for Teams/Slack, or Public URL to share a read-only version.

6

Use Ask AI

Query your database in plain language in the Ask AI tab. Try: "What are the top 3 items for my next digest?"

7

Assign Digest Sections

After clicking + Add to Digest on any article, a section picker appears. Use the ⇆ Move to… dropdown to assign it to: Headline of the Week, Federal Watch, Provincial Watch, Regulatory & Funding, or On Our Radar.

8

Filter by Category Tags

Use the tag chips above the article feed (TRC, DRIPA, Budget, Urgent, etc.) to instantly filter articles by category. Click any tag on an article card to filter to that category. Click All to reset.

Reference Guide
Definitions, colour codes, and feature explanations
Reference Guide
Sentiment Tags
🔴 Critical — Critical of / negatively impacts policy
🟢 Supportive — Positive / reinforces policy direction
⚪ Neutral — Informational / no clear stance
Relevance Scores (1–10 AI Scoring)
🔥 9–10 — Critical; brief-worthy, immediate attention
⭐ 7–8 — High relevance; strong digest candidate
📌 6 — Moderate; articles below 6 are filtered out
1–5 — Low relevance; filtered out automatically by AI
Age Badges
🟢 Today — Published today. Hover over this badge on any article card to see this tooltip. Today means the article was published or scraped within the last 24 hours — highest priority for review.
🟡 This week — Scraped within the last 7 days.
(No badge) — Older than one week.
Loading Your Groq API Key
PolicyPulse uses Groq AI (free, no credit card) to power Ask AI, article analysis, and AI summaries. To load your API key at any time:
1. Go to the Ask AI tab
2. Click the 🔑 Load API Key button in the top-right
3. Get a free key at console.groq.com → sign in → API Keys → Create Key
4. Paste the key (starts with gsk_) and click Save
The key is stored in sessionStorage (cleared when you close the tab — re-enter each session for privacy).
Citation Formats (3+)
PolicyPulse supports APA, MLA, and Chicago citation formats. Use the Citations tab to auto-generate a bibliography from any tracked article. The AI Auto-Generate button uses Groq to refine formatting to academic standards.
Article Side Colours
Cyan / Blue — Unread article. You haven't opened or reviewed this article yet. Disappears once you mark it read.
Gold / Amber — Staged for digest. You've added this article to your weekly briefing. It will appear in the Digest tab.
Red — Flagged for follow-up. Use the 🚩 Flag button to mark articles that need action, monitoring, or review later.
No colour — Read. You've already reviewed this article.
🚩 Flag Button
The Flag button (hover over any article card to reveal it) marks an article for follow-up with a red side bar. Use it for articles that need action — an upcoming consultation deadline, a bill you're tracking, or an article to bring to a meeting.

To see all flagged articles at once, click the 🚩 Flagged Only pill filter above the article feed. Flagged articles are never removed automatically.
+ Stage / Add to Digest
Clicking + Add to Digest on any article card (or + Digest in the article preview) stages it for your weekly briefing. The article gets a gold side bar and is automatically sorted into a digest section based on jurisdiction:

Briefing Note Worthy / Relevance 10 → Headline of the Week
Federal jurisdiction → Federal Watch
BC / Municipal → Provincial Watch
Everything else → Regulatory & Funding
You can manually move any article to a different section using the ⇆ Move to… dropdown in the Digest tab.
Notes — Article-Linked Notes & PDF Attachments
Notes can be linked to articles: click Open in Notes ↗ on any article preview to create a note pre-filled with that article's details.
PDF Attachments: Use 📎 Attach PDF to attach a downloaded article PDF to any note. Click the filename to open it in a new browser tab.
Export: Use 📄 Export PDF to save any note as a PDF, or export all notes as a .txt file.
✉ Sending the Digest
The Send Digest button in the Digest tab requires your Railway backend to be connected — it uses your backend's email service to deliver to subscribers. If you haven't connected a backend yet, use Copy Digest to copy the full text and paste it into an email manually, or use Export PDF to save a formatted version.

🔒 Privacy & Data
What PolicyPulse stores, where, and how to clear it
✓ Short answer: PolicyPulse stores everything locally in your browser only. No personal data is sent to any server. No accounts, no tracking, no ads.
Stored in your browser (localStorage)
The following data lives only on this device, in this browser. It never leaves your computer unless you choose to export it.
📝
Notes
All notes you write in the Notes tab, including any article-linked notes and PDF attachments. Stored under pp_notes_data.
📚
Citations
Your saved bibliography entries. Stored under pp_citations.
🤖
AI-generated summaries & Why It Matters
When you click Generate on an article, the result is saved locally so it persists if you close and reopen the article. Stored under pp_gen_analysis. The text is sent to Groq's API to generate the analysis — see the API Keys section below.
🔍
Keyword watchlist & saved searches
Keywords you add to the watchlist and any saved search filters. Stored under pp_saved_searches. If a backend is connected, watchlist keywords are also synced there.
⏰
Digest schedule
Your chosen day and time for auto-sending the digest. Stored under pp_schedule.
🌙
Theme preference
Whether you've chosen dark or light mode. Stored under pp_theme.
🔗
Backend URL
The Railway backend URL if you've entered a custom one. Stored under pp_api_base. This is only your own server address — no credentials.
⚠ Subscriber data — important
Subscriber names and email addresses are the only personal data in PolicyPulse that belongs to other people. They are stored in two places:
→
Your browser (localStorage) under pp_subscribers — so your list persists between sessions on this device.
→
Your Railway backend (SQLite) — if you've connected a backend, subscriber emails are synced there so digest sending works from the server.
→
Your SMTP provider (e.g. Gmail) — email addresses pass through your email server when digests are sent. This is standard for any email service.
Recommended practice: Only add subscribers who have explicitly agreed to receive your digest. You are the data controller for subscriber information — you are responsible for managing consent, honouring unsubscribe requests, and complying with applicable privacy law (e.g. CASL in Canada).
Stored for this session only (sessionStorage)
🔑
Groq API key
Your Groq API key is stored in sessionStorage only — it is automatically deleted when you close the browser tab. It is never written to localStorage, never sent to the PolicyPulse backend, and never logged. It is only used to make direct API calls from your browser to Groq's servers.
What is never collected
✕  No login or user accounts — you don't need to sign up to use PolicyPulse
✕  No usage tracking or analytics beyond basic Google Analytics page views (which you can block with any ad blocker)
✕  No cookies for advertising or cross-site tracking
✕  No personal data sent to the Railway backend — it only stores article metadata scraped from public government websites
✕  No data shared with or sold to third parties
Third-party services used
Groq API — When you use Ask AI or the Generate button, your query or the article text is sent to Groq's API for processing. Groq's privacy policy applies to those requests. Your API key is never stored on any PolicyPulse server.
Google Analytics — Basic page-view analytics (which tab you're on, when you opened the app). No personal identifiers. You can opt out with any browser ad blocker.
allorigins.win — Used as a proxy to fetch article content when you click Generate. The article's public URL is passed through this proxy. No personal data is involved.
Clear your data
All locally stored data can be cleared at any time. This will remove your notes, citations, saved searches, digest schedule, and generated analyses. This cannot be undone.

⚙️ How the Research Scraper Works
🔍 Step 1 — Keyword Matching
The scraper uses your Keyword Watchlist (from the Scraper tab) plus a set of default policy topics to query each database. It automatically targets terms like “Indigenous policy Canada”, “DRIPA UNDRIP”, “pharmacare”, and “post-secondary education Canada”.
📡 Step 2 — Multi-Database Query
Six open-access databases are queried in parallel: OpenAlex (250M works), Semantic Scholar (220M papers), PubMed Central (biomedical), DOAJ (peer-reviewed journals), arXiv (preprints), and 6 Canadian think-tanks. No API keys required.
🤖 Step 3 — AI Relevance Scoring
Every article is passed through Gemini AI which scores it 1–10 for relevance to BC/Canadian policy. Only articles scoring 6 or higher are saved. Low-relevance academic noise is automatically discarded.
📅 Step 4 — Scheduled Weekly
The research scraper runs every Monday at 8:00 AM (Vancouver time) automatically via the Railway backend scheduler. You can also trigger it manually with Fetch New Research above. Results appear in the Article Feed within ~90 seconds.
🔓
100% Open Access: PolicyPulse only indexes freely available research. Every article in the Research feed can be opened and read without a subscription. The scraper never touches paywalled content — JSTOR, Scopus, and Elsevier are explicitly excluded.
