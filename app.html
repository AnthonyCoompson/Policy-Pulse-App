Quick Start Guide
Get up and running with PolicyPulse in minutes
Quick Start Guide
1

Get Your Free Groq API Key

Visit console.groq.com → sign up → API Keys → Create Key. Free, no credit card. Sign in with Google, click "Create API Key". Paste it in the prompt when PolicyPulse loads to enable Ask AI.

2

Configure Your Sources

Go to Scraper → Sources to add or remove government/policy news sources. Click Scrape Now to fetch new articles on demand.

3

Set Up Your Keyword Watchlist

In Scraper → Search & Archive, add keywords like "pharmacare", "TRC", "DRIPA". Articles matching your keywords are surfaced automatically in the Dashboard Recommended panel.

4

Review the Article Feed

Click any article headline ↗ to open the source article in a new tab. Use ✓ Mark Read to track what you've reviewed, and ↩ Mark Unread to undo. Use + Add to Digest to stage an article.

5

Build & Send Your Digest

Staged articles auto-organize in Digest → Staging Area. Add "Why This Matters" notes, then click Send Digest, Copy for Teams/Slack, or Public URL to share a read-only version.

6

Use Ask AI

Query your database in plain language in the Ask AI tab. Try: "What are the top 3 items for my next digest?"

7

Assign Digest Sections

After clicking + Add to Digest on any article, a section picker appears. Use the ⇆ Move to… dropdown to assign it to: Headline of the Week, Federal Watch, Provincial Watch, Regulatory & Funding, or On Our Radar.

8

Filter by Category Tags

Use the tag chips above the article feed (TRC, DRIPA, Budget, Urgent, etc.) to instantly filter articles by category. Click any tag on an article card to filter to that category. Click All to reset.

Reference Guide
Definitions, colour codes, and feature explanations
Reference Guide
Sentiment Tags
🔴 Critical — Critical of / negatively impacts policy
🟢 Supportive — Positive / reinforces policy direction
⚪ Neutral — Informational / no clear stance
Relevance Scores (1–10 AI Scoring)
🔥 9–10 — Critical; brief-worthy, immediate attention
⭐ 7–8 — High relevance; strong digest candidate
📌 6 — Moderate; articles below 6 are filtered out
1–5 — Low relevance; filtered out automatically by AI
Age Badges
🟢 Today — Published today. Hover over this badge on any article card to see this tooltip. Today means the article was published or scraped within the last 24 hours — highest priority for review.
🟡 This week — Scraped within the last 7 days.
(No badge) — Older than one week.
Loading Your Groq API Key
PolicyPulse uses Groq AI (free, no credit card) to power Ask AI, article analysis, and AI summaries. To load your API key at any time:
1. Go to the Ask AI tab
2. Click the 🔑 Load API Key button in the top-right
3. Get a free key at console.groq.com → sign in → API Keys → Create Key
4. Paste the key (starts with gsk_) and click Save
The key is stored in sessionStorage (cleared when you close the tab — re-enter each session for privacy).
Citation Formats (3+)
PolicyPulse supports APA, MLA, and Chicago citation formats. Use the Citations tab to auto-generate a bibliography from any tracked article. The AI Auto-Generate button uses Groq to refine formatting to academic standards.
Article Side Colours
Cyan / Blue — Unread article. You haven't opened or reviewed this article yet. Disappears once you mark it read.
Gold / Amber — Staged for digest. You've added this article to your weekly briefing. It will appear in the Digest tab.
Red — Flagged for follow-up. Use the 🚩 Flag button to mark articles that need action, monitoring, or review later.
No colour — Read. You've already reviewed this article.
🚩 Flag Button
The Flag button (hover over any article card to reveal it) marks an article for follow-up with a red side bar. Use it for articles that need action — an upcoming consultation deadline, a bill you're tracking, or an article to bring to a meeting.

To see all flagged articles at once, click the 🚩 Flagged Only pill filter above the article feed. Flagged articles are never removed automatically.
+ Stage / Add to Digest
Clicking + Add to Digest on any article card (or + Digest in the article preview) stages it for your weekly briefing. The article gets a gold side bar and is automatically sorted into a digest section based on jurisdiction:

Briefing Note Worthy / Relevance 10 → Headline of the Week
Federal jurisdiction → Federal Watch
BC / Municipal → Provincial Watch
Everything else → Regulatory & Funding
You can manually move any article to a different section using the ⇆ Move to… dropdown in the Digest tab.
Notes — Article-Linked Notes & PDF Attachments
Notes can be linked to articles: click Open in Notes ↗ on any article preview to create a note pre-filled with that article's details.
PDF Attachments: Use 📎 Attach PDF to attach a downloaded article PDF to any note. Click the filename to open it in a new browser tab.
Export: Use 📄 Export PDF to save any note as a PDF, or export all notes as a .txt file.
✉ Sending the Digest
The Send Digest button in the Digest tab requires your Railway backend to be connected — it uses your backend's email service to deliver to subscribers. If you haven't connected a backend yet, use Copy Digest to copy the full text and paste it into an email manually, or use Export PDF to save a formatted version.
